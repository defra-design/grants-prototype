Who can apply

You can apply if you:

  • are a farmer or agricultural contractor whose business is registered in England
  • will do the grant-funded work in England
  • estimate the project costs are over £87,500

Agricultural contractors can only use the grant for buying robotics and innovation equipment.

What the grant is for

Slurry treatment equipment

The grant can pay up to 40% of the costs for:

  • mild acidification equipment (all projects must buy acid storage, dosing equipment, mixing tank and pump)
  • acidification infrastructure (any work to adapt or install pipework, pumps etc to get slurry into the acidification system and then out to storage)

Robotics and innovation equipment

The grant can pay up to 40% of the costs for:

  • robotic or innovative horticulture and arable equipment (for example, robotic weeding, advanced ventilation controls)
  • robotic or innovative animal welfare equipment (for example, robotic milking)
  • innovative LED lighting equipment (for example, wavelength-specific LED lighting for crop and livestock production)

The minimum grant you can claim is £35,000 (40% of £87,500). The maximum grant is £500,000.

If you want to apply for both a slurry project and a robotics project, you must submit 2 separate applications. The maximum grant amount for both projects together is £500,000.

When to apply

Grant stage Opens Closes
Check if you’re eligible 1 January 2022 28 February 2022
Full application 1 January 2022 31 May 2022

You must have applied for planning permission before you submit a full application. Any planning permission must be in place by 31 December 2022.

You will invalidate your application if you start the project or commit to any costs (such as placing orders) before you receive a funding agreement.

Before you start the project, you can:

  • get quotes from suppliers
  • apply for planning permission (this can take a long time)

How to apply for a grant

Check if your project can apply for a grant

You’ll need:

  • information about your business (for example, number of employees, turnover)
  • information about the project (for example, volume of slurry or digestate, the project impact)
  • a list of the items you’d like to buy for the project
  • an estimate of the total cost of the items

After you have gone through the checker, you’ll get a score showing how well your project fits the funding priorities.

RPA wants to fund projects that:

  • improve productivity
  • improve the environment
  • introduce innovation

When you are happy with your score, you can submit your details to the RPA.

You can go back through the checker as many times as you like but you can only submit your details once.

Check if your project can apply for a grant.

Submit a full application

The RPA will be in touch when the full application period opens. They will tell you if your project scored well enough to get the full application form.

You should read the Farming Transformation Fund Improving Farm Productivity grant manual before you submit an application.

If you submit an application, RPA will assess it against other projects and value for money. You will not automatically get a grant. The grant is expected to be highly competitive and you are competing against other projects.

If your application is successful, you’ll be sent a funding agreement and can begin work on the project.

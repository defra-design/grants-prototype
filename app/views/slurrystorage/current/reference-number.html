What happens next

  1. RPA will be in touch when the full application period opens to tell you if your project is invited to submit a full application form.

    2. If your project is invited to submit a full application, you need:

    • a storage calculation plan, which you will need to submit for review to the Environment Agency
    • confirmation that your project will comply with Water Resources (Control of Pollution) (Silage, Slurry and Agricultural Fuel Oil) regulations (SSAFO) and build standards (signed by you and your engineer)
    • a nutrient management plan
    • a declaration that you will comply with monitoring requirements
  2. If you submit an application, RPA will assess it against other projects and value for money. You will not automatically get a grant. The grant is expected to be highly competitive and you are competing against other projects.
  3. If your application is successful, you’ll be sent a funding agreement and can begin work on the project.

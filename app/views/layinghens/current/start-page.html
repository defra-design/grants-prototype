Use this service to:

  • check if you are eligible to apply for a grant for your project (takes about 5 minutes)
  • check how well your project fits the funding priorities (takes about 15 minutes if you have all the project details)

Who can apply

You can apply if you:

  • are a laying hen or pullet farmer
  • will do the grant-funded work in England

What you can apply for

You can apply for grant funding to replace or refurbish existing laying hen or pullet housing, or to add a veranda to laying hen or pullet housing.

If you are applying for grant funding to replace or refurbish housing, you can also apply for grant funding to add a solar photovoltaic (PV) system to the housing.

You must submit an application for each project. You can apply for grant funding for either housing projects or veranda projects.

You can apply for up to 40% of estimated costs of:

  • replacing or refurbishing laying hen or pullet housing
  • adding a veranda to existing housing

Funding for a solar PV system will be capped at the lowest of these 2 amounts, either:

  • the cost of 75m2 of the bird floorspace or
  • 25% of the invoice price for purchase and installation of the solar PV system

For laying hen or pullet housing and a solar PV system, the maximum grant you can apply for is £500,000. The minimum grant is £15,000 (40% of £37,500).

For adding a laying hen or pullet veranda only, the maximum grant you can apply for is £80,000. The minimum grant is £15,000 (40% of £37,500). The RPA will award the grant funding for verandas on a first-come first- served basis.

Before you start

To use the checker, you need:

  • information about your business (for example, number of employees, turnover)
  • information about the project (for example, building structure)
  • a list of the items you’d like to buy for the project
  • an estimate of the total cost of the items

If you do not enter any information for more than 20 minutes, your application will time out and you will have to start again.

Problems using the online service

If you have any problems using the online service, call the RPA and follow the options for the Farming Transformation Fund scheme.

The Farming Investment Fund (FIF) provides grants to improve productivity and bring environmental benefits. FIF is made up of 2 separate funds:

  • the Farming Equipment and Technology Fund
  • the Farming Transformation Fund

Farming Equipment and Technology Fund

This fund provides grants towards the cost of equipment and technology, for example horticulture, forestry, livestock and resource management, to improve the productivity of farms in a sustainable way.

How to apply

Round 1 applications open on 1 March 2022 and close at 31 May 2022. Read the FETF Round 1 manual for full details about who is eligible and what you can get funding for before you apply.

Apply now for an FETF grant

If you have any questions

Email FETFEnquiries@rpa.gov.uk or call the RPA helpline on 03000 200 301 and select the option for the Farming Equipment & Technology Fund.

Farming Transformation Fund

This fund provides grants towards large capital items to help businesses improve productivity, profitability, and environmental sustainability. There are 3 types of grant available from the Farming Transformation Fund. They are:

  • Water Management – open now for application
  • Improving Farm Productivity – open later this year for applications
  • Adding Value – open early 2022 for applications

Water Management grant

Apply online for funding towards capital items for:

  • the efficient use of water for irrigation
  • constructing on-farm reservoirs to improve water supplies for crop irrigation
  • adopting best practice irrigation application equipment

How to apply

Use the Water Management online checker tool to find out if you can apply for this grant.

Read the Farming Transformation Fund Water Management grant manual.

Improving Farm Productivity grant

Apply online for funding towards capital items for:

Slurry treatment equipment

  • mild acidification equipment (all projects must buy acid storage, dosing equipment, mixing tank and pump)
  • acidification infrastructure (any work to adapt or install pipework, pumps etc to get slurry into the acidification system and then out to storage)

Robotics and innovation equipment

  • robotic or innovative horticulture and arable equipment (for example, robotic planter, robotic weeding, egg-handling robots, advanced ventilation controls)
  • robotic or innovative animal welfare equipment (for example, robotic milking)
  • innovative LED lighting equipment (for example, wavelength-specific LED lighting for crop and livestock production)

How to apply

Use the Improving Farm Productivity online checker to find out if you can apply for this grant.

Read the Farming Transformation fund Improving Farm Productivity grant manual.

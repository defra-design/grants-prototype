Latest news

Slurry management grants available now. You can apply from 17 May 2021 to 28 June 2021. Your project must be completed before you can claim the funding. All claims for funding must be made before 31 March 2022.

Who can apply

Livestock, dairy, arable and horticultural farmers and also new businesses.

Only apply if the estimated costs are over £87,500. Grants can cover up to 40% of the eligible costs. The minimum grant you can claim is £35,000 (40% of £87,500).

What is the grant for

It can pay up to 40% of the costs for:

  • precision spreading equipment
  • best practice application equipment, such as dribble bars and trailing shoe systems
  • slurry digestate umbilical systems, tankers and storage bags
  • software and sensors to optimise slurry application like inline, real-time nutrient sensors, flow meters

How to apply for a large grant

  1. Think about what the business needs, develop a rough plan and estimate the project costs.
  2. Check if you can apply
  3. Make an expression of interest and wait to hear back. If it is successful, you will be invited to make a full application
  4. Make a full application. If this is successful, you’ll get a funding agreement
  5. Sign and return the funding agreement
  6. Start work on your project
  7. Pay for all the work
  8. Claim your grant money
